Information about Thien Mu Pagoda

Thien Mu Temple

The name of the temple means Temple of the Celestial Lady. It is a historic temple in the city. Its iconic seven-story Phuoc Duyen pagoda is regarded as the unofficial symbol. It is around 3.1 mi from the Citadel of Hue constructed by the Nguyen dynasty and sits on the northern bank of the Perfume River.

History

Build in 1601 on the order of the first Nguyen lords, Nguyen Hoang, who at that time was the governor of Hue. The original temple was simply constructed, then later expanded and refurbished. In 1665, major construction was undertaken by the Nguyen Lord Nguyen Phuc Tan.
