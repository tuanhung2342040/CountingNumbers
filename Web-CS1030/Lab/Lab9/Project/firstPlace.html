Information about Imperial city

The Imperial City

It is a walled enclosure within the citadel of the city of Hue, the former imperial capital of Vietnam during the Nguyen dynasty. It contains the palaces the housed the imperial family, as well as shrines, gardens, and villas for mandarins.

History

Constructed in 1803 under Emperor Gia Long as a new capital, it mostly served a ceremonial function during the French colonial period. After the end of the monarchy in 1945, it was damaged a lot because of the Indochina Wars.

Layout

The grounds of the Imperial City are protected by fortified ramparts 2 by 2 kms, and ringed by a moat. The water in the moat is routed from the Perfume River through a series of sluice gates.
